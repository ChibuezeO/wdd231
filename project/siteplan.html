Igbo Cultural Heritage Hub - Site Plan

Site Name

Name: Igbo Cultural Heritage Hub

Reason: The name reflects the website’s focus on celebrating and preserving the cultural heritage of the Igbo people from Nigeria’s South-East region (Anambra, Enugu, Imo, Abia, and Ebonyi states). It is clear, memorable, and directly tied to the project’s mission of promoting Igbo traditions, history, and community events, making it ideal for branding and recognition.

Site Purpose

The Igbo Cultural Heritage Hub aims to preserve and promote the rich cultural heritage of the Igbo people through an engaging website. It will provide:

  • Information on Igbo traditions, such as the New Yam Festival (Iwa Ji), marriage customs, and kola nut ceremonies.
  • Historical insights into pre-colonial Igbo societies and their modern contributions.
  • A community hub for upcoming cultural events like the Ofala Festival and Mmanwu masquerade events, with a platform for user-submitted stories.
The site will serve as an educational and interactive resource for Igbo people, Nigerians, and global audiences interested in Igbo culture.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: A young Igbo professional living abroad wants to learn about the New Yam Festival to connect with their roots. They ask, “What is the significance of the Iwa Ji festival, and how is it celebrated in different Igbo states?” The site’s Traditions & History page will provide detailed explanations and visuals.

Scenario 2: A tourist planning a trip to Enugu wants to attend a local cultural event. They ask, “What Igbo cultural events are happening in Enugu this month, and how can I participate?” The Community & Events page will list upcoming events like Mmanwu masquerade festivals with dates and participation details.

Color Schema

The color schema reflects Igbo cultural elements for a vibrant and cohesive look:

  • Forest Green (#006600): Used for backgrounds (e.g., header, footer) to symbolize the lush landscapes of Igbo land and the agricultural significance of the New Yam Festival.
  • Coral Red (#FF4040): Used for headings and accents (e.g., buttons, links) to represent the iconic red caps of Igbo chiefs and the vibrancy of cultural festivals.

Typography

The typography is clean and readable, balancing cultural resonance with modern aesthetics:

  • Lora (Serif): Used for headings (h1, h2, h3) to convey tradition and elegance, inspired by Igbo artistry.
  • Open Sans (Sans-Serif): Used for body text and navigation for clarity and accessibility across devices.
